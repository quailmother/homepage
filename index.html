Owner - The Wee Bee Beanery, Mobile Coffee Trailer 2017

I designed the Wee Bee’s branding from the ground up. It is a very personal project to me. One of my passions is Bee and pollinator health. I choose the name and the logo to highlight that. A percentage of the Wee Bee’s profits I donated to local pollinator gardens. I feel very strongly about healthy, nourishing foods. With this in mind, I created all my recipes from scratch, without sugar and with superfood ingredients. I made chai, turmeric latte and caramel latte from my own recipes. My coffee beans were sourced from ethically minded roasters, ran by women for 25 years. I installed bronze pourer Stands in the window, so customers could watch their coffee being made while I told them about the bean I was brewing. I had a weekly tip duel. Customers could put their tip into one of two jars betting on different duel outcomes. Winners were posted on Facebook. Everything was organic, the cups were compostable and the coffee sweetened with local honey. The trailer was tiny but filled with love.

Here is an article I wrote about it on steemit: My Experience Building And Running A Specialty Coffee Trailer, The Wee Bee Beanery.

Co-Owner - Boulder Creek Village Wash And Community Garden 2016 - 2017

I managed and co-owned a laundromat and drop off laundry service. We created a large community garden in the laundromats leach field. It hosted 18 beds and was the only sunlit patch around. People came from all around to grow tomatoes and sunflowers. While under my management laundromat was rebranded, painted, and the wasteful machines replaced with eco-friendly ones. I taught sustainable farming practices and water resource management. I organized a variety of events including seed and plant swaps, clothing trades and a Barter Fest to challenge people to find new ways of using the things they have lying around, to rethink what resources look like. We had a community compost pile anyone in the community could contribute to, finding new ways to lighten the load on landfill waste. Extra food was donated to the local branch of food not bombs in Santa Cruz.

Landscape Design 2015 - 2017

Landscaping, specializing in sustainable native plants as well as permaculture food guilds. I worked on an all women’s crew. We used all organic methods and sustainable practices. I had several clients in which I maintained their gardens and landscapes, pruned trees and improved systems to make them more environmentally friendly by planting native plants that need less water, drip systems so cut down on water waste. A key aspect of my design was planting in guilds, so each place helps support the other and lessens their dependency on chemical fertilizers and water. I spent a month living in the northern woods of Idaho, living off wild edibles and researching wild plant guilds to deepen my understanding and ecology of my design.

Dispatch - Catalina Harbor Patrol 2014 - 2015

Managed 700 mooring sites around Two Harbors and a large harbor patrol crew, the leases and day to day rentals, in 12 mooring sites located in different coves around the west side of the island. During the summer season, I managed the incoming dispatch calls on the radio in Two Harbors, Catalina Island, often 300+ a day, coordinated with the Coast guard and the hyperbaric chamber. Also coordinated search and rescue as well as vessels assist. And during the winter season, I would renew all leases with the leaseholders of 700 moorings in 11 coves. This was comprised of 50+ emails a day, keeping track of everyone's progress with timely followups and attention to detail. And above all nurturing strong relationships with all of the leaseholders and their families.
